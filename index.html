Transforming the Digital Frontier

Background

My case study focuses on the re-design for a government agency webpage: The Department of Interiors of the United States. The project was conducted independently.

Problems

Through user research, it was determined that the navigation is not intuitive for the majority of users. Upon arriving at the homepage, the majority of the users were unclear as to the purpose of the site.

Solution

Read onwards to see my solutions and design decisions.

My focus was dedicated to understanding the user and their needs, As such, I used numerous methodologies: development of Proto-persona, heuristic analysis, Annotations, usability studies, mood board and user flows (not all methods are presented in case study) .

Proto-persona

Age: 32. Gender: Female. Occupation: Environmental Scientist. Education: Master's degree in Environmental Science.

Scientist Sandy

Scientist Sandy currently works for a non-profit organization that focuses on preserving and protecting the environment. In her role as an environmental scientist, she is responsible for researching and analyzing environmental issues and developing solutions to address them. She is particularly interested in environmental policy and regulation, climate change and its impact on ecosystems, wildlife conservation and preservation, and sustainable practices and renewable energy.

Sandy’s Goals:

Stay up-to-date on the latest news and policies related to the environment and conservation efforts.

Sandy’s Frustrations:

Difficulty finding relevant information on the website due to cluttered navigation and overwhelming amount of content. Frustration with outdated or inaccurate information. Limited access to resources and tools she needs for her work.

Heuristic Evaluation

Usability Study

>

We had 5 participants complete 3 user tasks on the Government of Interior webpage.  The test was moderated, remote and held on May 10th and May 11th, 2023.  A peer, assisted me in  conducting usability tests and data collection.

Research Questions

How simple is it for users to navigate this task?  Determine how users navigate at the "Find a campground" page Determine how easy it is for users to navigate at the Volunteer Opportunities with using only the footer 

Synthasizing Data

Our research indicates that users have difficulty navigation the information hierarchy. Users expressed that there is an overwhelming amount of content

Top navigation (IA) design  Purpose of the webpage should be more clear Condense footer navigation  Make the carousel more visible on the desktop homepage  Make contact information easier to locate

Card Sorting

Because the finding focused on the hierarchy of information, I deployed the card sorting method to: define, group, and structure information into primary, secondary and tertiary navigation

Revision of sitemap

For my revized sitemap, I made changes to the top navigation bar. I changed the terminologies that didn't resignated with users and replaced them : ‘Newsroom’ has been re-named ‘Careers and Media’. I also revamped the footer by condensing navigation to keep it clean, this also addresses users insights that the original homepage had an overwhelming amount of optoipns.  

The above user flow is for the revized volunteer application process

Low Fidelity Prototyes and Testing Designs

As a user researcher,  I want to understand if the information architecture makes sense to the majority of users.

Research questions I have while preparing for usability testing:  Can multiple users successfully navigate to the prompted locations?  What are the common themes and insights from users?  Is the Header navigation easy to use?  A moderated, remote, usability study was held on June 10th & June 11th, 2023. I recruited 7 participants. All participants are adults (18+).   5 female and 2 male

Low Fidelity Prototypes

Next was testing the low-fidelity prototypes. User insights revealed: - Having an arrow drop-down icon for the tertiary navigation is not necessary. - Spelling errors need to be edited in both mobile and desktop footer navigation. - Footer for the mobile navigation has "too much white space" and should be closer to the subscription element.

High Fidelity Prototypes Iterations

The high-fidelity design for the navigation bar removed the unnecessary arrow icon from tertiary navigation I kept the underline for the current/hoover tab as 80% of users positively mentioned it without prompting. In the navigation bar, the initial ‘Careers & Media’ tab was confusing to users. During the usability study user were asked to complete the application to volunteer . My finding was that 60% of users did not understand that the volunteer section would be classified under ‘Careers”. So, further in my iterations i’ve addressed this issue by changing the terminology to: ‘Join Us’ .

Summery

Through the intial define phase, it was determined that the flow of information was not intuitive for the users. Users also had difficulty understanding what the webpage was for. Through the ideation phase, which included closed card sorting, and user task flows. I made changes to the flow of information on the navigation bar and footer. For the protoype phase, I constructed a responsive webpage for both desktop and mobile devices with the changes to the nav and footer. I then proceeded to testing my low-fidelity wireframes. The results indictaed improvment for given task flows. A take away from this usability study included the need to change terminologies that resignate with majority of users. With that information, I created the high-fidelity prototypes, user testing showed an increase in successful task completion with the changed terminologies and navigation links.

Next Steps

  • Clean up the Hi-Fi prototypes for both desktop and mobile (ensure all components are interacting correctly) 
  • Develop the Hi-Fi mobile menu for iterated prototype 
  • Change ‘Careers & Media’ to ‘Join Us & Media’ consistently in prototype components. Currently, some component still have the old terminology
  • To further intuitive navigation and information hierarchy, I would next test my assumption that: ‘Join Us & Media’ could be changed by having the  ‘Media’  portion separate from the ‘Join Us’. Further testing is required. A/B Testing on hero carousel images
